About the name: Alexandria was the biggest library in the world once upon a time. It was fashioned after Alexander the Great in Egypt after his death and in the town named for him. Exactly when in the Ptolemaic dynasty the library was built is shrouded outside our known history. When I learned about this library as a kid, I was in awe. I wanted to learn everything there was to learn in the world. 

In the popular mind, the burning of Alexandria happened at the height of its power. The vision of it falling from the highest height is so tragic, but ultimately wrong. Alexandria has been in decay for at least a century. Imagine that, being so mighty as to withstand a decade of decay. It started when Ptolemy VIII expelled all the foreign scholars from the city. The first alleged historical case of brain drain was the Huguenots from France, but I disagree. Xenophobia feels so good, but it burns so bad. With the expulsion went prestige, and with prestige went funding, and with funding went scholars. By the time Julius Caesar came along with a torch (the fucking dick), there was no original work coming out of the library. It was just a dusty place with scrolls capturing centuries of learnings. It hurts my heart to think about it.
